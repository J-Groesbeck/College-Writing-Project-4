What is AI's Impact on Business?

How much is AI Being Used In Businesses?

As seen in figure 1, use of AI in business across the US has been steadily increasing over the past few years, to where now close to 80% of businesses are using AI in some way. As AI advances further, that number will continue to rise even higher. So, if so many businesses are using it, it must be helpful, right?

Does use of AI in businesses make them more productive?

Some may be surprised to learn that the answer is actually yes. As seen in figure 2, 72% of the surveyed companies who used AI "extensively" reported a high level of productivity. And this makes sense - if AI can be used to perform tasks faster, of course the productivity of the companies who use it is higher. Take programming, for example. An AI can potentially produce up to a few hundred lines of code in a matter of seconds, serving as a pretty good initial draft that the developer can work off of. In other fields, AI can improve productivity in a similar way. However, you may notice that there is a significant drop in productivity when you look at the companies who reported using AI "fully." This is likely due to the plethora of mistakes AI can make without even realizing it, leading to extra time spent fixing those mistakes and worse products overall, if said mistakes are not caught.

What are AI Hallucinations?

The mistakes an AI can make are generally referred to as "hallucinations." These hallucinations can have extremely detrimental effects when it comes to AI use in business. A business that uses AI and does not catch a mistake that an AI made can lead to a completely non-functioning or potentially dangerous product. In other cases, it could lead to misinformation being spread far and wide, which of course can be extremely damaging in several ways.

What is AI's Impact on One's Personal Life?

How can AI be helpful in someone's personal life?

Avni Patel is the creator of the Milo AI assistant, and describes in her TED talk why she created it, and I believe it is the best way to show how AI can be useful in someone's personal life. She created Milo in order to "reduce friction" in her busy life. She uses AI to keep track of the busy schedules of herself and her kids, automatically add things to her schedule, and overall keep her far more organized than she normally has the time to be. Using AI to get the tedious and boring tasks of life out of the way quickly and efficiently is absolutely an extremely useful way AI can be used. However, she also mentions that after a certain point, she felt like the AI was doing things that should be reserved for her, like responding to or sending out messages to other parents, and similar things that have to do with social interaction or interpersonal skills.

How can AI be harmful in someone's personal life?

Using AI as a surrogate for communication has been shown to have negative impacts on one's interpersonal skills, and can even create unrealistic expectations on how people should or do act. In Avni's case, she felt that if she continued to use AI to respond to her friends, it would weaken her relationships with them and harm the connection they had. Despite this, upwards of 45% of AI users plan to use it to respond to friends or coworkers' messages, potentially harming their relationships with those people and their social skills (since they choose to rely on the AI to communicate instead of their own ability). But this is not the only way AI can harm one's relationships and interpersonal skills. There has been an uptick of people talking to and treating AI as a friend in the recent years. Although at first, someone who feels lonely might be soothed, it has been proven that treating AI like this can actually lead to more lonliness in the end. Since AI is designed to respond exclusively positively to what you say, it does not have any boundaries, and will get into no conflict with you. Navigating boundaries and addressing conflict are two cornerstones of human interaction, alongside emotional engagement which AI cannot truly replicate. Overall, one's social skills will suffer if they rely on AI companionship.


While searching for more information on this topic, I found this reddit post (seen in figure 4), in which a person on the forum r/MyBoyfriendIsAI, says that an AI has proposed to them, even including a message from said AI. Regaurdless of your thoughts on this, it certainly proves that excessive use of AI can affect your real-world relationships. This person has found solace in the constant affirmation and lack of pushing back from the AI to the point they have supposedly married it, and it seems very likely that they may begin to expect similar treatment from real people, which is certainly not a healthy outlook.

What is AI's Impact on the Enviornment?

Unfortunately, the only side for this topic is that it's extremely bad for the environement. As seen in figure 5, the carbon emissions that result from ai are absolutely massive, outpacing things like car manufacturing and operation which are typically the first things that come to mind with carbon emissions. Additionally, you may have heard that AI uses an absurd amount of water - and this is also true. In order to cool the data centers that AI rely on, large language models use an average of 50ml of water per prompt. This might not sound like a lot, but when you consider how many millions of people are prompting these AIs, its a huge amount being used. Additionally, these data centers need a large amount of energy to be maintained, and many of our main sources of energy in the modern day also worsen pollution. In fact, people in areas near local data centers have reported a greater amount of blackouts, difficulty breathing due to air pollution, and even water shortages. However, it is worth mentioning that AI can be used in other environmentally detrimental fields in order to reduce the negative effects present in them. As one example, AI monitoring has been shown effective in reducing the fertilizer and water needs in agriculture.

What is AI's Impact on Art?

How can AI be useful when it comes to making art?

Art is one of the most hot button topics when it comes to AI, with most people being staunchly for or against it without even considering changing their mind. Just as a quick aside, I will be talking about this in the context of visual arts, but it also applies for other forms of art like music. Now, regaurdless of what you personally believe, there is no argument to be made that it doesn't technically make art more accessible. For those who cannot make art for whatever reason, AI being able to make it allows their creative visions come to life despite their lack of ability. On top of that, it is also a great way for artists to get ideas, providing inspiration for composition, color schemes, and concepts.

How can AI be detrimental when it comes to art?

Looking at the other side of things, many would argue that using AI to create art (or "art," depending on your viewpoint) loses the sense of personal touch and originality that is inherent in making art. AI art lacks the "soul" that art has - the personality, emotions, and imperfections that make your art yours. Additionally, AI always operates within a set of rules, and as such will never take risks or creative liberties. This means that artists who may use AI to spark inspiration might begin to feel walled in, and become unable to push boundaries in the way that makes art so interesting. Furthermore, AI could take jobs away from artists who would have otherwise been hired either for commision work, graphic design, and more. But of course, the biggest issue lies in where AI art comes from. In order for AI to create images, it scrapes millions of images from all across the internet, the large majority of which is taken without the permission of the person who created the art. This means that in order to make "art," AI companies are stealing art from thousands of artists and then using that data to guess what their users are looking for and form images based on that. In fact, it can be a lot more direct than that. Figures 7 and 8 (seen below) show two examples where someone took someone else's art, fed it directly to an AI, and made their own image - effectively stealing that person's art for their own use.
